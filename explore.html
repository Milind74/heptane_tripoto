Rajasthan Tourism and Travel Guide

What captivates travellers about Rajasthan? We believe it’s the element of surprise. Rajasthan is one of the few places in the world where no two experiences are the same. Where on one trip you could be enjoying tea in a royal palace, on another you could be traversing the thick jungles of Ranthambore.... But, what remains same is the vibrant culture and the heartwarming hospitality of the locals. Located in western India, Rajasthan tourism promises memorable experiences for all kinds of travellers and you'd be surprised at the variety of things to do here. Home to the Pink City of Jaipur, the City of Lakes, Udaipur, the picturesque hill station of Mount Abu, the beautiful Ranthambore National Park and the land of sand dunes, Jaisalmer – Rajasthan tourism offers experiences that become part of your journey. While there are numerous ways to explore Rajasthan tourism, travellers believe a road trip is the most convenient. However, if you don't have too much time on your hands, you can always plan multiple trips to the state. Among the most popular places to see in Rajasthan tourism, we suggest including Jaipur, Ajmer, Chittorgarh, Udaipur, Jodhpur, Jaisalmer and the Shekhawati region in your itinerary. Since Rajasthan is a desert state, the temperature here is mostly dry and the best time to visit is undoubtedly between October and February. The days are pleasant and the nights are chilly, giving you the opportunity to spend time outdoors and explore tourist attractions.
